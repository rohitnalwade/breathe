Technical FAQ: Germ Filtration

Q: How effective are your purifiers at filtering germs like bacteria and viruses?

A: This is a critical question. Our service utilizes purifiers equipped with **True HEPA filters**, which are medical-grade filtration systems. Their effectiveness is certified to capture 99.97% of airborne particles at 0.3 microns (¬µm). Due to the physics of air filtration, they are highly effective across a broad spectrum of particle sizes, including those that constitute airborne germs. [4]

Bacteria

Most airborne bacteria (typically 0.5 - 5.0 ¬µm) are well within the capture range of our filters. This includes common pathogens like *Staphylococcus aureus* and *Streptococcus pneumoniae*.

Viruses

While individual virus particles are smaller (~0.1 ¬µm), they travel in larger respiratory droplets and aerosols which are easily captured. For the smallest particles, a mechanism called diffusion ensures they are effectively filtered. This includes Influenza viruses, Coronaviruses, and Rhinoviruses.
