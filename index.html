The Impact of Daily Pollution on Lung Health

The harm from air pollution isn't just a long-term risk; it's happening daily. Our system acts as a protective buffer against both immediate and chronic health effects.

Short-term exposure to PM2.5 can decrease a child's lung capacity by up to 33.7 mL for every 10 µg/m³ increase in daily pollution. [2]

"Research... found that children living in communities with high levels of PM2.5 had slower lung growth, and had smaller lungs at age 18 compared to children who lived in communities with low PM2.5 levels." – California Air Resources Board [3]
